Synk — Product Vision Document
One-Page Alignment for the Team

Vision Statement
All your saved content—browser tabs and X bookmarks—unified in one intelligent library that organizes automatically, searches by meaning, and never loses anything.

The Problem




Who We Serve
Research-heavy professionals, content creators, and knowledge workers who live in browsers and on X/Twitter—typically 24-45 years old, digitally overwhelmed, and already paying for productivity tools that don't solve this.
The Pain
"I know I saved this somewhere but I have no idea where." Content is scattered between browser tabs and X bookmarks. Finding anything requires luck. Manual organization fails within weeks.
Current Reality
68% of users report tab anxiety. Average knowledge worker has 25+ tabs open. X's bookmark feature hasn't improved since 2018—still just a chronological dump with zero search. Browser crashes destroy hours of research. Deleted tweets vanish forever.
The Cost
Hours lost re-finding content. Valuable research disappears permanently. Users have saved thousands of items across platforms but can retrieve maybe 5% when needed. Digital memory is fragile and fragmented.


The Solution




What We're Building
A web app + Chrome extension that unifies browser tabs and X content into one intelligent, searchable library.
Core Value Proposition
Save once. Find instantly. Never lose anything. Zero manual organization required.
Key Capabilities
Unified Capture — One-click tab saving, automatic X sync


AI Organization — Automatic categorization, tagging, and grouping


Semantic Search — Find by meaning ("that thread about hiring"), not keywords


Permanent Archive — Content survives browser crashes, deleted tweets, account suspensions


Why Now
Factor
Explanation
Market Shift
PKM market exceeds $6B annually, growing 25% YoY. Users already pay $15-30/month across fragmented productivity tools. "Digital hoarding" is a recognized challenge.
Technology Enabler
AI capabilities now enable automatic organization and semantic search at consumer-grade quality and cost. What required manual effort is now automatable.
User Behavior
X has 550M+ monthly users. Power users save 50-200+ items monthly with no way to search them. The gap between saving and finding has become unbearable.



Success Definition
Timeframe
Target
90-Day Milestone
Chrome extension live with one-click tab capture, basic semantic search, and X OAuth connection working. 1,000 beta users actively saving content.
12-Month Vision
20K weekly active savers. 10K cross-source users (both tabs and X connected). 5% paid conversion. Time-to-find under 15 seconds for any saved item.
North Star Metric
Time to Find — Seconds to locate any saved item. Target: <15 seconds (Year 1) → <5 seconds (Year 3)


Guiding Principles
Principle
What It Means
Capture should be invisible
The user's only job is to indicate "this matters." Saving takes one click; X syncs automatically. We handle everything else.
Organize for me, not with me
No human should manually categorize content in 2026. AI does it better, faster, and actually does it consistently. Manual organization is a failed paradigm we reject.
Nothing disappears
Digital permanence is a feature. Deleted tweets, dead links, crashed browsers—none of it should cost you your saved knowledge. What you save stays saved.





What We're NOT Building
Anti-Goal
Why
A note-taking app
We feed PKM tools (Notion, Obsidian), not compete with them. We're capture and retrieval, not creation.
A read-later service
We're not optimizing reading experience. We're solving findability across sources.
A social media manager
We don't help you publish. We help you capture and retrieve what you consume.
A complex workspace tool
No learning curve. No configuration required. It works on day one or we've failed.
